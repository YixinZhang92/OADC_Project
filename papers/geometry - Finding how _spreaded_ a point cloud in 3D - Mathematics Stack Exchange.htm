I don't understand your gripe with standard deviation. However, here's something that should work:

Let {(𝑥𝑖,𝑦𝑖,𝑧𝑖):𝑖=1,…𝑛}{(xi,yi,zi):i=1,…n} be a collection of points. Let (𝑥,𝑦,𝑧)=1𝑛∑𝑛𝑖=1(𝑥𝑖,𝑦𝑖,𝑧𝑖)(x,y,z)=1n∑i=1n(xi,yi,zi) be the centroid. We can take

𝜎2=∑𝑛𝑖=1((𝑥−𝑥𝑖)2+(𝑦−𝑦𝑖)2+(𝑧−𝑧𝑖)2)𝑛σ2=∑i=1n((x−xi)2+(y−yi)2+(z−zi)2)n
𝜎=𝜎2‾‾‾√σ=σ2 gives you the standard deviation in Euclidean distance of all points from the centroid, which should be exactly what you're looking for.
